    WHO DO I THINK I AM?

    Isabella Hodges is a senior graphic design student at San Diego State University. She is involved at many organizations on and off campus but she prides herself her school’s student-run newspaper “The Daily Aztec” as Graphics Editor, managing print and designs published on the website.

    In the future, Isabella hopes to work for NGOs, media publication or branding for businesses. Take a look at a timeline of her work below!

    ABOUT ME

    My Design Timeline

    SPRING ‘23

    FROM SCRATCH

    I began attending San Diego State University in 2022 but did not start attending design classes until after the first semester. The only experience I had with graphic design was with a couple of years of experience in highschool. I liked it so much I decided to continue working on my skills.

    Typography 1 and Graphic Design 1 were my first ever university level classes. It felt super official to have professors push you to think outside of the box and to keep on hatching ideas. It was kind of intimidating to be surrounded in such a talented and fast-paced enviornment at first. I eventually found my footing by practicing text layouts and projects catered to composition and branding.

    SPRING ‘24

    MEDIA EXPERIMENTS

    At this time I was not taking design classes but I still involved myself in activities that pushed for creative problem-solving. I just joined The Look fashion club on campus. I created a magazine spread for American Club Kids; a fashion style that was used as a political statement by the queer youth in the 80s. It was one of my first time collaborating with a team of writers, photographers and models to create a piece all would like.

    I also began to pick up photography. The quick moments that usually flash by were hard but rewarding to capture. I would eventually take a class that would teach me the foundational camera skills but I did well considering I just began creating.

    FALL ‘24

    EXPANDED HORIZONS

    I knew there was more to do with design besides doing the assigned work in class. I began involving myself in clubs and internships outside the scholarly hemisphere. I spent the time outside of class at publications on campus like the Daily Aztec Newspaper and The Look Fashion Magazine. I also became a Design Intern for the Coronado Historical Association where I co-designed an exhibit and attendance cards for walking tours.

    I think my involvement in these clubs and organizations really sparked my interest for news coverage and community betterment; where I hope to work one day. Giving back to the community and seeing it's helpful impact is what motivates me to design. Though I am scared to leave these aspects of my life behind when I graduate, I know that will make a impact.

    SPRING ‘25

    PRETTY IN PINK

    For some reason during this time I was heavy-handed on using pink in my designs - there was no problem and still isn't for liking pink- I think it has to do with avoiding the color for a good portion of my life.

    However this is beside the point, during this semester I took Typography 2 and Illustration. I was first introduced to figma and application design when I created a convention-like event for Women Designers and Typographers. It was very helpful to consider scale and accessibility; in previous classes this topic was not explored as heavily. With illustration it was cool to explore of what Illustrator was capable of with creating an enviornment based solely on shapes.

    SPRING ‘26

    THE FINAL TOUCH

    As my time at San Diego State University comes to a conclusion, I feel grateful for all the experiences I was able to be a part of and the friends and teachers I had the honor of sharing time here with. Even though many hours of working on projects have passed by I still feel that there is still more that I want to learn about graphic design and all that encompass it.

    Even though it is my last semester, I am still finding new mediums to apply my designs. I am currently learning CSS, HTML and JavaScript; which helped build this website. I also am interested in printing designs using Risograph.
